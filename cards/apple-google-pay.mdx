Your Glide card works with Apple Pay and Google Pay from the moment it's issued. The virtual card is in your wallet seconds after you tap **Add to Apple Pay** or **Add to Google Pay**.

## Add to Apple Pay

<Steps>
  <Step title="From the Glide app">
    Tap **Cards &rarr; pick your card &rarr; Add to Apple Pay**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve in the Wallet sheet">
    iOS shows the standard Apple Wallet sheet with your card preview. Tap **Add**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify with your bank">
    iOS may ask you to confirm via a one-time code or biometric prompt back in the Glide app. This is Apple's standard flow; the verification finishes in seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Your card is now in Apple Wallet. Hold it near a contactless terminal, double-click your side button, and pay.

## Add to Google Pay

<Steps>
  <Step title="From the Glide app">
    Tap **Cards &rarr; pick your card &rarr; Add to Google Pay**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve in the Google Wallet sheet">
    Android shows the standard Google Wallet sheet. Tap **Continue**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm with your bank">
    Google Wallet may verify via a one-time code or biometric. The flow takes seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Your card is now in Google Wallet.

## Online payments

Apple Pay and Google Pay work for in-app and online checkouts on supported sites and apps. The same card credentials are used &mdash; merchants never see your real PAN, just a token that's tied to your device.

## Watch out for

- **Region locking** &mdash; Apple and Google may region-lock the wallet to your phone's primary region. If you're a digital nomad with a phone registered in one country and a Glide account in another, contact us if Apple Pay refuses to provision; we'll diagnose with you.
- **Family sharing** &mdash; Apple Cash and Google Pay's family-sharing features don't share Glide cards. Issue a separate card for each family member instead.
- **Watch and wearables** &mdash; once your card is in Apple Pay or Google Pay on your phone, you can extend it to Apple Watch and Wear OS devices through the standard wallet flow.

## Tokenization

Every Apple Pay and Google Pay transaction uses a **device-specific token** instead of your real card number. If your phone is stolen, you remove the device token from your iCloud or Google account; the underlying Glide card stays unaffected.
